<p><p>Through-Silicon Vias (TSVs) are vertical interconnects that pass through a silicon wafer or die to connect stacked integrated circuits. There are two main types of TSV markets: 2.5D TSVs, which involve stacking multiple dies on a silicon interposer, and 3D TSVs, which involve stacking multiple dies directly on top of each other. 2.5D TSVs are commonly used in high-performance computing and networking applications, while 3D TSVs are often used in mobile devices and other compact electronics to increase performance and reduce footprint.</p></p>
