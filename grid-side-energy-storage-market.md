<p><p>Grid-side energy storage refers to the use of energy storage systems to manage and optimize the electricity grid. Peak-to-valley arbitrage involves storing excess energy during low-demand periods and selling it during peak-demand times. Stored energy can be used to provide backup power during outages or to supplement renewable energy sources. Peak shaving involves reducing peak demand charges by deploying stored energy during times of high electricity prices. Frequency modulation helps stabilize the grid by adjusting power output to match fluctuating demand.</p></p>
