<p><p>Cell based seafood, also known as lab-grown seafood or cultured seafood, is produced using cellular agriculture techniques where cells are taken from a real seafood source, such as fish or shrimp, and then grown in a lab setting to replicate the taste and texture of traditional seafood products. This process eliminates the need for traditional fishing or aquaculture, reducing the environmental impact and ethical concerns associated with conventional seafood production.</p><p>The Cell Based Seafood Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 4.5% during the forecast period. Factors driving the growth of the market include increasing consumer awareness about sustainability and animal welfare, rising demand for alternative protein sources, and technological advancements in cellular agriculture. Moreover, the growing concerns about overfishing and ocean pollution are also boosting the demand for cell-based seafood products.</p><p>The latest trends in the cell-based seafood market include collaborations between food companies and biotech firms to develop innovative products, investments in research and development to enhance the taste and nutritional value of cell-based seafood, and partnerships with retailers and restaurants to expand distribution channels.
