<p><p>Transcatheter pulmonary valve replacement is a procedure used to replace a faulty pulmonary valve without open-heart surgery. There are two types of valves used in this procedure: Balloon-Expanded and Self-Expanded. Balloon-Expanded valves are inflated with a balloon during the procedure, while Self-Expanded valves deploy themselves once in position. Both types offer advantages such as less invasive surgery, shorter recovery times, and reduced risk of complications compared to traditional open-heart surgery, making them popular choices in the market.</p></p>
