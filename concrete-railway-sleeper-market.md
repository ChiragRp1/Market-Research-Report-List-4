<p><ul><li>Longitudinal Sleepers</li><li>Transverse Sleepers</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Concrete railway sleepers are divided into two main types: longitudinal sleepers and transverse sleepers. Longitudinal sleepers are laid parallel to the tracks, providing stability and support to the rails. They are commonly used in high-speed rail lines for their durability and resistance to wear and tear. On the other hand, transverse sleepers are positioned perpendicular to the tracks and are mainly used in urban or metro rail systems. They offer efficient track alignment and easy maintenance, making them ideal for shorter distance rail lines.</p></p>
