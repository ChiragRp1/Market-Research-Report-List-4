<p><ul><li>Spunmelt Nonwovens</li><li>Spunbonded Nonwovens</li><li>Spunlace Nonwovens</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Nonwovens for hygiene products are commonly categorized into three main types: Spunmelt Nonwovens, Spunbonded Nonwovens, and Spunlace Nonwovens. Spunmelt nonwovens are made by melting and extruding polymer granules to form continuous filaments that are then laid down to create a web. Spunbonded nonwovens are produced by spinning continuous filaments and then bonding them together. Spunlace nonwovens are created by entangling fibers with high-pressure water jets. Each type of nonwoven material offers unique properties and benefits for hygiene applications.</p></p>
